This memo synthesizes key findings about Llama small language models (SLMs) from a variety of sources. <h4>Key Findings</h4>

<h5>Extended Context Size Capabilities</h5>

Llama SLMs have the ability to handle extended context sizes of over 8,000 without the need for fine-tuning. This is a significant advantage as it allows for more complex and nuanced language processing tasks [Source: SLMEval_2023, Chunk: SLMEval_2023_chunk_0093].

<h5>Comparable Performance</h5>

The performance of Llama SLMs is on par with other models such as MiniCPM-MoE, which has 4 billion activated parameters. This suggests that Llama SLMs are capable of delivering high-quality results, despite their smaller size [Source: MiniCPM_2024, Chunk: MiniCPM_2024_chunk_0008].

<h5>Expanding Use and Performance</h5>

The use of Llama SLMs is expected to continue expanding, which will further enhance their learning capabilities and overall performance. This trend indicates the growing recognition of the value and potential of SLMs in the field of artificial intelligence [Source: DataQuality_2024, Chunk: DataQuality_2024_chunk_0098].

<h4>Conclusions</h4>

Llama SLMs demonstrate significant potential in the field of artificial intelligence. Their ability to handle extended context sizes without the need for fine-tuning, coupled with their comparable performance to larger models, makes them a valuable tool for various language processing tasks. As their use continues to expand, it is expected that their learning capabilities and overall performance will further improve.
