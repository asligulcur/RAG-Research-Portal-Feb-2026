<h4>Executive Summary</h4>

This memo synthesizes information from various sources to identify the company most active in the development and deployment of Small Language Models (SLMs). The analysis reveals that OpenAI, Google's DeepMind, and Microsoft Research are the most active players in this field. However, OpenAI and Microsoft Research are particularly notable for their contributions to the development of SLMs.

<h4>Key Findings</h4>

<h5>OpenAI's Contributions to Small Language Models</h5>

OpenAI has been instrumental in the development of SLMs. The company's researchers have contributed to the development of the Phi series, a notable example of SLMs [Source: MiniCPM_2024, Chunk: MiniCPM_2024_chunk_0009]. The Phi series is distinguished by its capacity for deployment on end-user devices, such as personal computers and smartphones, even in the absence of a GPU. OpenAI's researchers, including Mojan Javaheripi, S´ebastien Bubeck, Marah Abdin, Jyoti Aneja, Caio C´esar Teodoro Mendes, Weizhu Chen, Allie Del Giorno, Ronen Eldan, Suriya Gunasekar, Piero Kauffmann, Yin Tat Lee, Yuanzhi Li, Anh Nguyen, Gustavo de Rosa, Olli Saarikivi, Adil Salim, Shital Shah, Michael Santacroce, Harkir, have been involved in the development of these models [Source: Phi3_2024, Chunk: Phi3_2024_chunk_0048].

<h5>Google DeepMind's Contributions to Small Language Models</h5>

Google's DeepMind has also been active in the SLM space. The company's Gemma 2 project aims to improve open language models at a practical size [Source: Gemma2_2024, Chunk: Gemma2_2024_chunk_0057]. Key personnel involved in this project include Jeff Dean, Demis Hassabis, Koray Kavukcuoglu, Clement Farabet, Elena Buchatskaya, Sebastian Borgeaud, Noah Fiedel, Armand Joulin, Kathleen Kenealy, Robert Dadashi, and Alek Andreev [Source: Gemma2_2024, Chunk: Gemma2_2024_chunk_0056].

<h5>Microsoft Research's Contributions to Small Language Models</h5>

Microsoft Research has also made significant contributions to the development of SLMs. Researchers Ronen Eldan and Yuanzhi Li have explored how small language models can be and still speak coherent English through their project, TinyStories [Source: TinyLlama_2023, Chunk: TinyLlama_2023_chunk_0000].

<h4>Conclusions</h4>

While several companies are active in the development of Small Language Models, OpenAI and Microsoft Research stand out for their significant contributions to this field. Google's DeepMind is also active in this space, but its contributions are not as clearly defined as those of OpenAI and Microsoft Research.
