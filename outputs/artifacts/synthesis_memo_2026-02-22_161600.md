<h4>Executive Summary</h4>

This memo synthesizes the different approaches to model compression discussed across the corpus. The approaches include the use of Bits Per Character (BPC) as a metric for evaluating compression efficiency, the application of quantization methods to compress large language models (LLMs) with minimal accuracy loss, and the use of Length-MAX tokenization for superior compression performance. The memo also highlights the importance of model compression in managing the high memory demands of large models.

<h4>Key Findings</h4>

<h5>1. Bits Per Character (BPC) as a Compression Metric</h5>

BPC is a quantitative metric used for evaluating the compression efficiency of large language models (LLMs). It measures the cross-entropy between the model's predicted distribution and the true character distribution. When the predicted distribution aligns perfectly with the true distribution, this cross-entropy formulation becomes mathematically equivalent to lossless compression, making BPC a theoretically grounded metric for quantifying how effectively LLMs compress given text [Source: Farseer_2025, Chunk: Farseer_2025_chunk_0093].

<h5>2. Quantization Methods for Compressing Large Language Models</h5>

Quantization methods are used to compress LLMs with minimal accuracy loss. GPTQ, an approximate second-order method for quantizing large language models, can accurately compress some of the largest publicly-available models down to 3 bits per value on average [Source: GPTQ_2022, Chunk: GPTQ_2022_chunk_0041]. This method is task-agnostic and uses a tiny amount of randomly-chosen data for calibration, thus avoiding significant ethical implications [Source: GPTQ_2022, Chunk: GPTQ_2022_chunk_0043].

<h5>3. Length-MAX Tokenization for Superior Compression Performance</h5>

Length-MAX tokenization consistently achieves better compression than frequency-based baselines. It reduces Tokens Per Character (TPC) by 14-18% compared to BPE, SentencePiece, and WordPiece across a range of vocabulary sizes [Source: LengthMAX_2025, Chunk: LengthMAX_2025_chunk_0023]. Unlike BPE, which builds tokens incrementally through merge operations, Length-MAX selects tokens directly based on their value in representing the corpus, avoiding the creation of potentially wasteful intermediate tokens [Source: LengthMAX_2025, Chunk: LengthMAX_2025_chunk_0062].

<h4>Conclusions</h4>

Model compression is a critical aspect of managing the high memory demands of large models. Different approaches, including the use of BPC as a compression metric, the application of quantization methods, and the use of Length-MAX tokenization, offer promising solutions for effective model compression. Further research and development in these areas could lead to more efficient and cost-effective deployment of large language models.
