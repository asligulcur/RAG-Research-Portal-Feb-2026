<h4>Executive Summary</h4>

The field of language models has seen a significant shift towards smaller, more efficient models. While large language models (LLMs) have been the focus of research in recent years, the financial and operational burdens associated with their training and deployment have led to a renewed interest in small language models (SLMs). These models, typically not exceeding 7 billion parameters, are distinguished by their capacity for deployment on end-user devices, such as personal computers and smartphones, even in the absence of a GPU. The latest trends in SLMs include the use of scalable training strategies, the use of synthetic data for post-training, and a new paradigm for the evaluation of language models.

<h5>Deployment on End-User Devices</h5>

SLMs are distinguished by their capacity for deployment on end-user devices, such as personal computers and smartphones, even in the absence of a GPU [Source: MiniCPM_2024, Chunk: MiniCPM_2024_chunk_0009].

<h5>Use of Synthetic Data for Post-Training</h5>

The use of synthetic data for post-training that has been filtered with various content safety filters could provide another opportunity for improving the overall safety of the models [Source: Orca2_2023, Chunk: Orca2_2023_chunk_0076].

<h5>New Paradigm for the Evaluation of Language Models</h5>

A new paradigm for the evaluation of language models has been introduced, which uses GPT-4 to grade the content generated by these models as if those were stories written by students and graded by a (human) teacher [Source: TinyLlama_2023, Chunk: TinyLlama_2023_chunk_0002].

<h4>Conclusions</h4>

The latest trends in small language models indicate a shift towards more efficient and practical models. The use of scalable training strategies, the ability to deploy on end-user devices, the use of synthetic data for post-training, and a new paradigm for the evaluation of language models are all indicative of this trend. These developments suggest that the future of language models lies in smaller, more efficient models that can be deployed in a variety of practical scenarios.
